Common questions

Things attorneys ask before they sign up.

Who is the legal practitioner of record on a YiLo matter?
Always the admitted attorney at your firm. YiLo is a software platform — we never act as the practitioner. Every document we produce is reviewed, signed, and filed under your firm's admitted attorney, in compliance with the Legal Practice Act 28 of 2014.
Is my client data POPIA-compliant?
All client data is stored on AWS af-south-1 (Cape Town), encrypted at rest with AES-256, with row-level access logging. We sign a standard POPIA Operator Agreement with every firm. An Information Officer is designated. Cross-border transfer never occurs without explicit, recorded consent.
Does YiLo touch trust funds?
No. YiLo never holds or moves client funds. Billing runs through your existing accounting system — Xero, Sage, or LegalSuite. YiLo produces invoices and ledger exports formatted for the Legal Practitioners' Fidelity Fund, but every cent stays in your trust account.
What happens if a calculation is wrong?
Every YiLo calculation shows its workings — the Act, the section, the figure, the source. When a court or the Master queries a number, the document already tells them where it came from. The admitted attorney remains responsible for verifying outputs before filing; YiLo is the tool, not the lawyer.
How does "pay per matter" actually work?
A matter is billed once when you generate the first statutory document — a J294, a Schedule of Damages, an L&D account. From that point on, every revision, regeneration, and supporting document for that matter is included. No surprise charges. No per-document fees. You only pay when you bill.
